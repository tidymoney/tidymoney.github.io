Emergency funds, in plain English

How much should my emergency fund be?

The standard guideline: 3–6 months of essential expenses. Three months if your income is steady and you could find work fast; six or more if income is variable, you're self-employed, or one paycheck covers the household.

What counts as "essential"?

What you'd still pay if income stopped tomorrow: housing, utilities, groceries, insurance, transportation, minimum debt payments. Streaming and takeout don't count — in a real emergency, you'd cut them.

Where should I keep it?

A high-yield savings account is typical: reachable in a day or two and earning interest, but separate enough that you won't spend it by accident. The point is availability, not returns.

Assumes contributions at the start of each month and monthly compounding (APY ÷ 12). Educational tool, not financial advice.
